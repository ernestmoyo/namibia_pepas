### Dear CEO,

The reconstitution of Namibia's national airline presents a unique opportunity: to build **performance monitoring and accountability infrastructure from the start**, rather than retrofitting it years later.

Hoop Africa Foundation's research across Namibian commercial SOEs revealed a consistent pattern — entities launch with ambitious strategies but lack the digital tools to track, measure, and course-correct. This leads to the cycle of underperformance, bail-out requests, and loss of public trust.

**PEPAS breaks that cycle by embedding accountability from inception.**

### What PEPAS Delivers for a New Aviation SOE

| Feature | Value |
|---------|-------|
| **KPI Framework From Day One** | Standardised performance indicators aligned to the entity's mandate, NDP6, and OPM expectations — established before the first quarter |
| **Quarterly Reporting** | Structured digital submissions from the first operating quarter — no Excel spreadsheets, no ad-hoc reporting |
| **Fiscal Risk Monitoring** | Track cash burn rate, subsidy utilisation, revenue ramp-up, and operating margins in real-time |
| **Governance Agreements** | Digital performance contracts between the board and the shareholder — compliant with the PE Governance Act |
| **Corrective Actions** | Formal escalation workflows for any KPI that falls below target — accountability built in, not bolted on |

### Why This Matters

Air Namibia's history is a cautionary tale of what happens without performance monitoring. The successor entity has the chance to be the **model SOE** — one that demonstrates accountability to government and the public from its first day of operations.
