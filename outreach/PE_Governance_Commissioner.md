### Dear Commissioner,

The **Public Enterprises Governance Act (2019)** mandates performance measures, governance agreements, M&E frameworks, and annual reporting for all SOEs. Despite this legislative foundation, **none of these requirements are operationalised in practice**.

This is not a new finding. The **JSA/JICA-sponsored M&E workshop** convened through the Ministry of Public Enterprises explicitly identified monitoring and evaluation as a **national-level gap across the entire public sector**. Our own primary research — 40 interviews with SOE executives, managers, audit, HR, legal, and operations teams — confirmed this at entity level:

- SOEs track strategy with **Excel spreadsheets and Gantt charts**
- Board evaluations are reduced to **attendance records and peer reviews**
- Performance management systems are either **partial (executive level only) or non-existent**
- Consequences for non-performance are **absent** — "There is no clear curtain to say if you have not implemented, what happens to you"

**Hoop Africa Foundation built PEPAS to close this gap — translating the Act's requirements into a working digital platform.**

### How PEPAS Maps to the Act

| Act Requirement | PEPAS Module |
|----------------|-------------|
| Performance measures & M&E measures (Section 4) | **KPI Library** — Standardised, comparable indicators aligned to NDP6, Vision 2030, HPP II |
| Written governance agreements (Section 14) | **Performance Contracts** — Digital template with approval workflows and version control |
| Annual reports within 6 months (Section 24) | **Compliance Tracker** — Automated deadlines, status pipeline, public disclosure hub |
| Criteria for performance evaluation | **Quarterly Scorecards** — Structured reporting with evidence, variance explanations, corrective actions |
| Board accountability and oversight | **Evaluation Tool** — Outcome-based assessment across 5 dimensions replacing superficial peer review |

### What Sets This Apart

This is not a theoretical framework designed in isolation. Every module maps directly to a **specific gap identified through primary field research** in Namibian SOEs. The researcher, Fanuel Dangarembizi, conducted this work as part of a **PhD study at the University of Johannesburg** — ensuring academic rigour alongside practical application.
