### Dear CEO,

The Development Bank of Namibia has the **most mature performance management system** among the SOEs we researched — a software-based Balanced Scorecard fully cascaded to all staff, quarterly reviews with consequences, and a sustainability model with clear parameters.

Your systems work internally. But as Namibia moves toward **centralised SOE oversight**, every entity will need to report into a **standardised national framework**. PEPAS is that framework — and DBN's existing maturity means adoption would be seamless.

### What PEPAS Adds Beyond DBN's Internal Systems

| Feature | Value to DBN |
|---------|-------------|
| **National Portfolio Context** | See DBN benchmarked against all 12+ major SOEs — demonstrating leadership to government |
| **Standardised Government Reporting** | Meet incoming centralised reporting requirements through structured quarterly submissions |
| **Fiscal Risk Benchmarking** | DBN's liquidity, NPL ratios, and capital adequacy compared against sector peers |
| **Compliance Visibility** | DBN's strong annual reporting record made visible as a national benchmark |
| **Governance Agreement Portal** | Digital performance contracts ready for the new OPM oversight structure |

### From Our Research

During our interviews with your executives, we noted DBN's emphasis on strategic planning rigour — five-year rolling plans, external facilitation, and clear alignment with the bank's sustainability model. PEPAS was designed to **codify exactly this kind of best practice** and make it the national standard.

DBN's participation would not only benefit the bank but **set the benchmark** that other entities are measured against.
